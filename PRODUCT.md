# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Recruiters, hiring managers, and potential clients who land here to evaluate Daniel as a full-stack, Web3, and mobile developer. They have roughly 60 seconds to form an impression before deciding whether to reach out. The job: move them from "interesting" to "I want to talk to this person."

## Product Purpose
Daniel Ezet Onoriode's personal portfolio. Showcases who he is, what he builds, and how he thinks across full-stack web, Web3 (EVM + Solana/Rust), and mobile (React Native). Success is an inbound message.

## Brand Personality
Technical, confident, ambitious. The physical object: a precision-machined component that also happens to look good — tight, considered, no filler. Not corporate. Not template. Built by someone who cares about craft at every layer.

## Anti-references
Generic Tailwind-starter portfolios. Overdone glassmorphism. LinkedIn resumes reformatted as a page. Anything that could have been generated from a template in 90 seconds. The saturated "dark + Inter + blue-pill buttons" developer portfolio look.

## Design Principles
1. Craft signals credibility — the quality of the UI is itself evidence of the engineering skill being sold.
2. Clarity before decoration — every visual element earns its place; no filler motion, no padding copy.
3. Momentum — the page should feel alive and purposeful without being distracting or exhausting.
4. Breadth without blur — full-stack + Web3 + mobile is a genuine differentiator; the design should carry that ambition without losing focus.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA minimum. Reduced-motion support for all JavaScript-driven animations (GSAP). Keyboard navigable.
